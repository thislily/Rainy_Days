Shipping Information

At Rainy Days, we offer shipping to customers throughout Norway and to some international locations, depending on our mood.

We ship orders via Regular Post, Delivered with Groceries, and New Startup Drivers. Most orders are processed and shipped within 1-2 business days of receipt. Shipping times vary depending on your location. In general, orders should not take more than 6 months for delivery. Shipping rates vary by country and general vibe of the drivers that day.

Returns or Exchanges

At Rainy Days, we strive for our customers to be completely satisfied with their purchases. If for any reason you are not satisfied with your Rainy Days product, you can return it within 30 days of purchase for a full refund or exchange. The item must be in its original condition, with all tags and packaging intact.

To initiate a return or exchange, simply contact our customer service team and provide your order number and the reason for the return. We'll provide you with a prepaid shipping label for your convenience. Once we receive your returned item, we'll process your refund or exchange promptly.

Please note that any items marked as "final sale" cannot be returned or exchanged. Additionally, we cannot accept returns or exchanges for items that have been worn, washed, or damaged after purchase.

We stand behind the quality and durability of our products and are committed to ensuring our customers are happy with their Rainy Days purchases.

Fabric Care and Maintenance

Proper maintenance of hiking jackets is essential to ensure their longevity and effectiveness in protecting you from the elements. Firstly, always follow the care instructions on the label. Before washing, zip up all zippers and close all Velcro tabs to prevent damage to the fabric. Use a mild detergent, and avoid fabric softeners and bleach. Wash the jacket on a gentle cycle in cold water, and hang it to dry, avoiding direct sunlight or high heat. If the jacket has a waterproof membrane, use a special detergent and reapply the DWR (Durable Water Repellent) coating as needed. Regularly check for any tears, holes or signs of wear, and repair them promptly to prevent further damage. By taking good care of your hiking jacket, it will last longer and continue to serve you well on all your outdoor adventures.

DIY Repairs

Repairing jackets yourself is a great way to save money and extend the lifespan of your gear. For small tears or holes, you can use a patch or repair tape to cover them. To repair a ripped seam, turn the jacket inside out, and use a needle and thread to stitch it back together. For a broken zipper, you can purchase a replacement zipper and replace it yourself by carefully removing the old zipper and sewing in the new one. When repairing your jacket, be sure to use the correct tools and materials, and take your time to ensure a neat and sturdy repair. However, for more significant repairs or if you are unsure of how to proceed, it's best to take your jacket to a professional tailor or outdoor gear repair specialist.
