More Than Just A Jacket

Pushing the Comfort Zone

In 1997, a group of outdoor enthusiasts and environmentalists came together to create a new kind of outerwear company, one that would prioritize sustainability and eco-friendliness in all aspects of its operations. They called their company Rainy Days.

Rainy Days' founders believed that clothing could be both functional and environmentally conscious, and they set out to prove it. They sourced materials from sustainable and ethical suppliers, used recycled and organic fabrics whenever possible, and designed their products to last for years rather than just a season.

At first, Rainy Days struggled to find a foothold in the competitive outerwear market. But as more and more consumers became aware of the environmental impact of fast fashion and disposable clothing, Rainy Days' message began to resonate.

Looking Forward

By the early 2000s, Rainy Days had become a leading brand in sustainable outerwear. They partnered with environmental organisations to raise awareness and funds for conservation efforts, and their products were worn by outdoor enthusiasts and eco-conscious consumers alike.

Today, Rainy Days continues to innovate in sustainable design and is recognised as a leader in environmentally friendly outerwear. Their commitment to the planet remains at the heart of everything they do.
